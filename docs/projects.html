Our Projects

Research initiatives and collaborative projects in mobile GIS for archaeology

The Mobile GIS SIG is involved in various research projects aimed at testing, developing, and improving mobile technologies for archaeological fieldwork. Our projects focus on establishing best practices, developing new tools, and creating workflows that enhance the efficiency and accuracy of archaeological survey.

Application Testing Program

Ongoing

Technical testing of newly available mobile GIS applications to evaluate their effectiveness in archaeological fieldwork. We assess usability, data quality, integration capabilities, and field deployment scenarios.

Testing Field Work Quality Assurance

Standards Development

Ongoing

Collaborative effort to establish standards and guidelines for mobile GIS usage in archaeology. This includes workflows, data formats, quality metrics, and security protocols.

Standards Guidelines Documentation

Tool Integration Research

Ongoing

Exploring integration between mobile GIS and other archaeological tools including GNSS receivers, geodetic equipment, drones, and various sensors to create comprehensive survey solutions.

Integration GNSS Drones

Crowdsourcing Quality Assessment

Research

Research project examining the quality, precision, and accuracy of crowdsourced archaeological data. Developing methodologies to validate and verify user-contributed information.

Crowdsourcing Data Quality Validation

Archaeological App Development

Planning

Long-term initiative to develop new mobile applications specifically designed for archaeological field survey. Working to influence industry and create tools that meet archaeologists' needs.

Development Mobile Apps User-Centered Design

Data Security Framework

Research

Developing frameworks and best practices for securing archaeological data in mobile environments, addressing concerns about data loss, theft, and appropriate public access.

Security Privacy Data Protection
